Foundations of Theoretical Neurocience

In Spring 2020, I'll be organizing a student seminar on the foundations of theoretical neuroscience. We'll begin by following the Dayan & Abbott (2003) textbook, and then progress to papers and other resources. This is loosely inspired by the MIT BCS Philosophy Circle. Especially in the second half of the seminar, we will focus on language processing.

Optional homework assignments are listed for each week, for those who are interested in implementing models and solving some math problems. [Update 1/22/20: links to these homeworks are currently missing, and we are attempting to recover them.]

Tentative Syllabus

Part I: Dayan & Abbott (2003)

Week 1: Introduction to Model Neurons
  • Dayan & Abbott, Chapter 1 "Neural Encoding I: Firing Rates and Spike Statistics"
  • Dayan & Abbott, Chapter 5.1-5.4 "Model Neurons I: Neuroelectronics" (up to Integrate-and-Fire)
Homework (Build an Integrate-and-Fire Model, DiffEq questions optional)
Week 2: Hodgkin-Huxley Model
  • Dayan & Abbott, Chapter 2 "Neural Encoding II: Reverse Correlation and Visual Receptive Fields"
  • Dayan & Abbott, Chapter 5.5-5.9 "Model Neurons I: Neuroelectronics" (Hodgkin-Huxley, Synapses)
Homework (Build Hodgkin-Huxley Model)
Week 3: Modelling Conductance
Week 4: Advanced Topics in Model Neurons
Week 5: Network Models I
Week 6: Network Models II

Part II: Additional Readings
