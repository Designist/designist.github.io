Nicholas Tomlin

I study computational semantics and machine learning. In particular, I'm interested in the intersection of formal linguistic theory and computational models of interaction. Some questions that interest me:

  • Are neural language models (e.g., RNNs) learning compositional structure? How can we do better?
  • Can NLP systems benefit from a Montagovian semantics? What are the computational equivalents of "possible worlds"?
  • How can we use pragmatics to build scalable, interactive language-learning systems?

In general, I'm interested in NLP methods that perform well on small datasets. Throughout my work, I hope to build models and systems which are successful on low-resource languages.


Current Research

Emergent Communication and Reinforcement Learning

  • Studying how agents may cooperate to develop a "language" in certain multi-agent reinforcement learning environments, and how to induce more natural linguistic structure via information theoretic concepts such as noisy-context surprisal.
  • To be presented at 2018 NeurIPS Emergent Communication Workshop. [Abstract]
  • Ongoing work in pursuit of Brown University honors thesis.

Pragmatic Dialogue Agents for Negotiation

  • Studied how the rational speech acts (RSA) model may be used to achieve goal-oriented dialogue in neural conversational agents. Built pragmatic agents for a negotiation task. [Code]
  • Work done at Stanford CSLI under NSF REU (award #1659585).
